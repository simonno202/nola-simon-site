description: "On letting go of the thing that defined you before you knew what you were doing."
---

There's a jacket I used to wear to every client meeting. Structured shoulders. Serious. The kind of thing you put on when you need to be taken seriously before you've opened your mouth.

I burned it. Not literally — though I thought about it. I donated it to a church sale and watched someone else walk out wearing the version of me that no longer fits.

The jacket was doing a job I didn't need it to do anymore.

This is about that. About the things we carry into rooms that used to be load-bearing and aren't anymore. About what it costs to keep wearing them anyway.
